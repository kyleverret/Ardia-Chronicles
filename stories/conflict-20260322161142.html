Shadow Legion Field Commander Defects to Grand Council with Maps of Hidden Supply Routes

Commander Varen Duskhollow claims senior officers receive orders from 'something that doesn't speak like a person.' Intelligence officials scrambling to verify.

DA
By Dorian Ashveil War & Conflict Reporter
22 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

THORNWALL GARRISON — Commander Varen Duskhollow of the Shadow Legion's 3rd Outrider Company crossed into Grand Council territory yesterday morning under flag of parley and surrendered himself, his command staff of four officers, and a leather satchel containing what intelligence officials are calling the most significant captured documents of the current conflict. I spoke with him for thirty minutes before military handlers ended the interview. His hands did not shake. His voice did not waver. But his eyes carried something I have seen before — in soldiers who have witnessed things they cannot yet name.

"I did not defect because we were losing," Duskhollow told me, his words measured and deliberate. "I defected because I no longer understood what we were fighting for." The 3rd Outriders were responsible for the Ashenmoor Pass ambush I reported on three days ago — an attack that destroyed a Grand Council supply column and killed seventeen soldiers. Duskhollow confirmed his unit executed that operation. He expressed no pride in it. "Those were clean orders. Military objectives, supply interdiction, acceptable losses. That is war." He paused. "What came after was not war."

The commander's documents include detailed maps of Shadow Legion supply caches throughout the contested Eastern March, patrol schedules for the next three weeks, and — most significantly — written orders bearing command seals that intelligence officers I spoke with could not identify. "The seal is not in our records," admitted Captain Sera Millbrook, the garrison's senior intelligence analyst. "It matches nothing from the Shadow Legion's known command structure." This aligns with testimony from the Shadow Legion defectors who surrendered at Thornwall last week, who claimed a "mysterious commander" had arrived and changed their orders. Duskhollow says he has seen this commander. He would not describe what he saw.

What he would describe was the change in orders following that commander's arrival. Duskhollow's unit was redirected from military targets to what he called "acquisition missions" — raids on sites with no apparent strategic value. A shepherd's shrine in the highland passes. An abandoned watchtower on the Greyward's edge. A village well that locals claimed had healing properties. "We were told to secure these locations and hold them until specialist teams arrived," he said. "We were not told what the specialists were looking for. We were told not to ask." Three of his soldiers asked anyway. They were transferred to units Duskhollow has been unable to locate since.

The reports of identical nightmares among wounded soldiers — which I documented in my previous dispatch — found confirmation in Duskhollow's account. His own troops experienced the same phenomenon after occupying a secured site near the Miravel Woods' northern boundary. "They dreamed of silence," he said. "Of a voice that was not a voice, asking questions they could not remember upon waking." Two soldiers in his company stopped sleeping entirely. One walked into the Greyward three nights ago and has not returned. The garrison's field medics are currently evaluating Duskhollow and his officers for similar symptoms.

I asked Duskhollow what he wanted the people of Ardia to understand. He was quiet for a long moment. "The Shadow Legion's soldiers believe they are fighting for independence from Grand Council authority," he finally said. "Most of them still believe that. I believed it." He looked at the maps spread before him, routes and positions that might cost hundreds of his former comrades their lives. "But someone is using that belief. Someone is moving us like pieces on a board toward objectives that have nothing to do with territory or politics." He met my eyes directly. "Find out who. Before whatever they are building is finished." Military intelligence has transferred Duskhollow to a secure location. I was not permitted to follow. But I have copies of three of his maps, and I intend to verify his claims personally. Something is directing this war toward ends that neither side's soldiers understand. The silence spreading through our forests, the drainage of ancient magic, the dreams that will not let wounded men rest — the pattern is there, if anyone chooses to see it.
