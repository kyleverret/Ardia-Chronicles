Grand Army Field Surgeons Report Identical Wounds on Dead From Both Sides of Shadow Frontier

Medics treating casualties from last week's Ashenmoor engagement discover matching blade patterns suggesting single weapon source.

DA
By Dorian Ashveil War & Conflict Reporter
22 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

IRONKEEP — I have spent nine years covering this realm's conflicts. I have never seen military healers as disturbed as the ones I interviewed yesterday at Forward Station Twelve, three miles south of the Ashenmoor contested zone. They asked me to look at something. I wish I hadn't.

The wounds are identical. Not similar — identical. Seventeen Grand Army soldiers killed in last week's supply column ambush bear slash patterns that match, down to the millimeter, wounds found on eleven Shadow Legion fighters recovered from the same engagement. Chief Field Surgeon Mira Thornwood, no relation to the Praxis magistrate, has served twenty-two years in military medicine. She told me she has never encountered anything like it. "Different bodies, different armor, different positions on the field," she said. "But the cuts are the same. Same angle. Same depth. Same slight curve at the terminus." She paused. "It's as if one blade killed them all."

I examined the bodies myself. The scar on my cheek — the one that glows near active battlefield magic — burned steadily the entire time. Whatever killed these soldiers carried enchantment. The same enchantment, applied with the same hand, to enemies wearing both the Grand Council's silver and the Shadow Legion's black. Commander Brin Ashfall of the 1st Praxis Legion confirmed that her scouts recovered no weapon matching the wound profile from the battlefield. No witnesses survived from either side's forward positions where the bodies were found.

Three sergeants I trust told me, separately, that similar wound patterns appeared on casualties from the Thornwall engagement earlier this month — casualties from both armies. The reports were filed. Nothing came of them. When I requested access to those medical records through official channels, I was informed they had been classified under the Shadow Frontier Military Authorization Act's emergency provisions. The same act currently being debated in the Grand Council. The same act Lord Commander Severin Drath insists will give the military the tools it needs to end this conflict.

I am not suggesting conspiracy. I am stating facts: someone or something is killing soldiers on both sides of this war with the same weapon, and the people with authority to investigate have chosen instead to hide the evidence. The medics at Forward Station Twelve deserve answers. The families of the dead deserve truth. I intend to find both.
