Shadow Legion Prisoners in Ironkeep Speak Same Words at Same Moment Across Isolated Cells

Seventeen captured soldiers, held separately without contact, simultaneously recite coordinates to location Grand Army maps show as empty.

DA
By Dorian Ashveil War & Conflict Reporter
23 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

IRONKEEP — At the Hour of Deep Night two days ago, seventeen Shadow Legion prisoners held in separate cells across Ironkeep's detention complex spoke aloud in perfect unison. Guards on three different levels of the fortress reported hearing the same words at the same moment: a string of numbers that correspond to map coordinates in the Thornback Mountains, followed by a single phrase in a language none of the jailers recognized. I have confirmed this account with four independent witnesses. The prisoners have not spoken since.

Commander Brin Ashfall of the 1st Praxis Legion permitted me access to the detention records on condition I not publish the specific coordinates until military scouts return from investigating the site. "These men were captured in three separate engagements over the past month," she told me. "They have never been housed within earshot of each other. They have received no visitors. There is no natural explanation for synchronized speech." The prison mages have examined all seventeen prisoners and found identical residue of an enchantment they cannot identify — something woven into the soldiers' minds that activated remotely and simultaneously.

I interviewed two of the prisoners through cell doors. Neither remembered speaking. Both appeared genuinely confused when told what they had said. One, a young man who surrendered at Thornwall claiming his unit's orders had changed after a mysterious commander arrived, asked me if I knew what the words meant. When I told him the numbers were map coordinates, the color drained from his face. "He showed us a map once," the soldier whispered. "The new commander. He said we were all carrying something important home, even if we didn't know it." He could not describe the commander's appearance. None of the defectors can. They remember a voice and a presence, nothing more.

The location indicated by the coordinates lies in the same region where Ironvein Mining Consortium recently abandoned three excavation sites — the area Vael Moonstrike has documented as the "Breathing Ground." When I brought this connection to Commander Ashfall, she was silent for a long moment before saying only: "The scouts leave at dawn." I have requested permission to accompany them. That request remains under consideration.

What troubles me most is not the synchronized speech itself, strange as it was. It is what followed. Within an hour of the incident, three of the prisoners began weeping without explanation. One kept repeating that he was sorry. Another asked the guards to kill him before "it wakes up." The prison healers sedated all three. They remain unconscious. I have covered this conflict for nine years, and I have seen what dark magic does to soldiers on both sides of the Shadow Frontier. I have never seen men more afraid of something inside themselves than of any enemy they might face in battle. Someone put something in these soldiers' minds — something that used them to deliver a message. The question now is who received it, and what they intend to do with coordinates pointing to ground that, according to multiple witnesses, has begun to breathe.
