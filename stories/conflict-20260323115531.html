Shadow Legion Deserters Report Officers Executing Soldiers Who Question Orders

Three separate groups crossing Grand Council lines describe identical purges. Field medics confirm wounds consistent with close-range execution.

DA
By Dorian Ashveil War & Conflict Reporter
23 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

IRONKEEP FORWARD POSITION — In the past seventy-two hours, nineteen Shadow Legion soldiers have crossed Grand Council lines at three separate points along the Shadow Frontier, each group telling the same story: officers are killing their own troops. I spoke with seven of them yesterday at a field hospital three miles south of the front. Their accounts match in ways that suggest either coordinated deception or shared truth. I have been doing this work long enough to recognize the difference.

A sergeant who gave his name only as Varn described what he called 'the questioning purge' that began nine days ago. 'Anyone who asked why we were holding position instead of advancing. Anyone who wondered aloud about supply routes that made no sense. Anyone who spoke to the newer commanders wrong.' He paused, looking at his hands. 'They took them behind the ridge. We heard the spells. Nobody came back.' Varn crossed the line with six others from his unit. Four more tried to follow. He heard the killing-spells behind him as he ran.

Field medics at the hospital confirmed that several deserters arrived with wounds consistent with close-range magical execution — the kind that leave burn patterns only visible when a target is kneeling. Commander Brin Ashfall of the 1st Praxis Legion, who has been coordinating deserter processing, told me the pattern matches intelligence she has been gathering for weeks. 'Something changed in their command structure around Frosthold,' she said. 'The soldiers who cross now are different from the ones we captured six months ago. These men are terrified — not of us, but of their own officers.'

The deserters describe a new commander who arrived at Shadow Legion forward positions approximately two months ago — a figure none of them have seen clearly, who issues orders through intermediaries and who has systematically replaced experienced officers with what Varn called 'the quiet ones.' When I asked what that meant, another deserter, a younger man with a fresh burn scar across his neck, answered instead. 'They do not blink right. They do not sleep. And when they speak, sometimes their mouths move before the words come, like they are remembering how to talk.'

I have covered the Shadow Legion conflict since its beginning. I have interviewed prisoners, documented battlefield conditions, and reported on the strange synchronicities that have emerged among captured soldiers. But this is the first time I have seen Legion troops willing to die crossing open ground rather than remain with their own forces. Whatever is happening on the other side of that frontier, the soldiers who lived it believe death at Grand Council hands is preferable to remaining under their new command. That should concern everyone responsible for deciding how this war ends.
