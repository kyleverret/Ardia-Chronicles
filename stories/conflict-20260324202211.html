Shadow Legion Prisoners Across Ironkeep Begin Speaking in Unison Again—This Time Reciting Grand Army Oath of Service

Forty-three captives in isolated cells simultaneously repeated the oath word-for-word. All were confirmed dead on Grand Army rolls.

DA
By Dorian Ashveil War & Conflict Reporter
24 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

IRONKEEP — I was present when it happened. Forty-three Shadow Legion prisoners, held in separate wings of the Ironkeep military stockade with no line of sight or sound between them, opened their mouths at the same moment yesterday evening and began reciting the Grand Army's Oath of Service. Not fragments. Not approximations. The complete oath, in perfect unison, including the unit designation of the 7th Eastern Regiment—a formation officially destroyed three years ago at the Battle of Ashenmoor Pass.

The guards I spoke with afterward were reluctant to describe what they witnessed. 'I've been on stockade duty for eleven years,' said Sergeant Marris Coldwell, a veteran of two Shadow Frontier deployments. 'I've heard prisoners scream, threaten, beg. I've never heard them pray to something I couldn't see.' She was referring to the final portion of the recitation—after the standard oath concluded, the prisoners continued speaking for another thirty seconds in a language none of the guards recognized. The stockade's duty mage, who asked not to be named, confirmed the secondary language contained 'structured magical resonance' but could not identify its origin.

This marks the second recorded instance of simultaneous speech among isolated Shadow Legion prisoners. The first occurred four days ago, when captives spoke identical words at the same moment across separated cells. But that incident involved only seven prisoners and lasted mere seconds. Yesterday's event was six times larger and continued for nearly two minutes. More troubling still: every prisoner involved bore unit tattoos matching Grand Army records that list them as killed in action.

I requested comment from Commander Brin Ashfall of the 1st Praxis Legion, who has been coordinating prisoner processing at Ironkeep since the spring offensive. Her response was uncharacteristically brief: 'Something is speaking through these soldiers. I don't know what, and I don't know why it chose our oath. But I intend to find out before it speaks again.' When I pressed for her assessment of the 7th Eastern connection, she shook her head. 'Ask the people who signed their death certificates. I only deal with the living—and I'm no longer certain these men qualify.'

The Grand Army's Northern Command has restricted all further access to the stockade pending what a spokesperson called 'enhanced security protocols.' My request to interview the prisoners directly was denied. But I managed to observe their cells before the lockdown took effect. The men inside sat motionless on their bunks, eyes open, breathing steadily. None responded to questions. None seemed aware of my presence at all. Whatever spoke through them yesterday is no longer speaking—but the silence it left behind felt, to this reporter, like the silence before an order is given.
