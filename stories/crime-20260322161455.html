Court Clerk Who Filed Vanthorn Financial Records Found Dead in Sealed Archive Room

Mirela Ashford's body discovered among documents she had organized for Magistrate Thornwood. Archive's protective wards showed no breach.

NX
By Nyx Shadowmere Crime & Justice Reporter
22 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

Mirela Ashford, forty-three, senior records clerk of the Praxis Central Court and the individual responsible for organizing the Vanthorn financial evidence now central to Magistrate Thornwood's smuggling prosecution, was found dead yesterday morning in Archive Chamber Seven — a secured room accessible only to court officers with sealed-document clearance. The chamber's protective wards, which log every entry and exit, recorded no visitors between Ashford's arrival at the ninth bell two nights ago and the discovery of her body sixteen hours later.

I examined the ward logs myself, having cultivated access to such records during my years covering this court. The enchantments are maintained by the Temple of the Radiant Dawn under the same covenant that now shelters the Vanthorn witnesses. They cannot be bypassed without leaving trace signatures that temple auditors would detect. They recorded nothing. Either Mirela Ashford died alone of causes yet undetermined, or someone possesses the capability to move through sacred wards without detection — a possibility that should concern everyone currently trusting those wards to protect the three remaining witnesses.

Ashford had served the court for nineteen years. Her colleagues describe her as meticulous, private, and utterly devoted to the integrity of court records. She was not a witness in the Vanthorn case. She was something potentially more valuable: the person who understood how every piece of documentary evidence connected. Two days ago, she sent a personal message to Magistrate Thornwood — contents unknown — requesting a private meeting. That meeting was scheduled for this morning. She kept detailed personal notes in a leather journal she carried everywhere. The journal was not found with her body.

This marks the fifth death connected to the Vanthorn prosecution. Marek Thornwell, strangled in his cell. Officer Ashcroft, killed by shadowbind venom. Three vanished witnesses who would likely be dead if not for temple sanctuary. And now a clerk who touched every financial document that might reveal who in Praxis has been profiting from Vanthorn's smuggling operation. Captain Sera Blackwood's office has claimed jurisdiction over this investigation as well. When I requested comment, I was informed that preliminary findings suggest Ashford suffered "acute magical exhaustion consistent with prolonged archive work." I have worked in those archives. I have never heard of anyone dying from organizing ledgers.

Magistrate Thornwood issued a brief statement this afternoon, her first public remarks since granting sanctuary to the witnesses. "Mirela Ashford dedicated her life to ensuring that truth could be preserved and found when justice required it," she said. "I will not permit her work to have been in vain." The Magistrate has requested temple healers examine Ashford's remains rather than the city's medical examiner — the same office that certified Thornwell's death as natural. High Priestess Alara Dawnmere has agreed. Whatever the healers find, I suspect it will not match Captain Blackwood's preliminary assessment.

The Vanthorn witnesses are scheduled to testify in six days. Someone is killing everyone who can illuminate the full scope of this conspiracy, and they are doing so with apparent impunity inside the very institutions meant to deliver justice. I knew Mirela Ashford. She helped me locate sealed case files on three separate occasions over the years, always following proper procedure, always insisting that the public's right to understand their courts mattered. She deserved better than to die alone in a room full of evidence that someone desperately wants to remain unexplained. I intend to explain it anyway.
