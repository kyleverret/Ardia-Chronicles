Magistrate Thornwood's Temple Sanctuary Breached; One Witness Dead, Two Others in Critical Condition

Attack on sacred grounds marks first violation of Temple Covenant in over two centuries. High Priestess Alara Dawnmere demands Council emergency session.

NX
By Nyx Shadowmere Crime & Justice Reporter
22 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

The Temple of the Radiant Dawn's protective sanctuary failed last night. Witness Jorek Vanthorn — nephew of the accused smuggler and the prosecution's key testimony on financial networks — is dead. The two remaining witnesses, whose names remain sealed by court order, are being treated for what temple healers describe as severe corruption wounds. I arrived at the temple grounds before dawn, having received word from a source I will not identify. The eastern courtyard's ward stones were not bypassed. They were shattered — fragments of consecrated marble scattered across thirty feet of sacred ground, still smoking with residual dark energy when I examined them.

High Priestess Alara Dawnmere addressed a small group of reporters at the Hour of First Light, her composure visibly strained. "The Temple Covenant has protected those who sought sanctuary within our walls since Year 624," she said. "In two hundred and twenty-three years, no force has broken our wards by violence. What entered our grounds last night was not mere magic. It was something that should not exist in this world." When I pressed for specifics, Dawnmere confirmed that temple archivists are researching the energy signatures left behind. She would say only that they bear similarities to reports from the Shadow Frontier — reports I have read, and which describe weapons that corrupt flesh on contact.

Captain Sera Blackwood arrived with a full Watch contingent shortly after I did. Her officers attempted to establish a perimeter around the temple, which Dawnmere refused to permit. "The Watch has no jurisdiction on consecrated ground," the High Priestess stated, her voice carrying clearly across the courtyard. "Given recent events, I have no confidence that Watch involvement would serve the interests of justice." It was as direct an accusation as I have heard from temple leadership. Blackwood withdrew her officers to the street but has stationed them at every exit. She declined to comment when I approached, though one of her lieutenants — a young officer I have cultivated as a source over several months — confirmed that Blackwood received a priority runescript message approximately two hours before the attack occurred.

Magistrate Thornwood issued a statement from an undisclosed location this morning, her first public communication since Mirela Ashford's death in the sealed archive room. "The prosecution of Marcus Vanthorn will proceed," she wrote. "The testimony of the surviving witnesses has been recorded under oath and preserved through temple protocols that predate the Grand Council itself. Those who believed they could silence this case by killing its witnesses have miscalculated." The statement did not address whether the remaining witnesses can recover sufficiently to appear in court. Temple sources tell me their prognosis is uncertain.

Six people connected to the Vanthorn case are now dead. Three Watch officers involved in the original arrest have requested transfers to distant postings. The court clerk who understood how the evidence connected is gone. And now the temple sanctuary that should have been inviolable has been breached by something carrying Shadow Frontier signatures into the heart of Praxis. I have spent six years covering this city's criminal networks, and I have never seen an operation this bold, this coordinated, or this willing to cross lines that even the Obsidian Ring has historically respected. Someone is protecting the Vanthorn smuggling network with resources that exceed anything a river shipping operation should command. I intend to find out who — and I intend to publish their names before anyone else dies for knowing them.
