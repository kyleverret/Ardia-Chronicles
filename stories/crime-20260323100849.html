Watch Commander Found Dead Hours After Receiving Subpoena to Testify on Crown Reserve Security Protocols

Renna Blackshore was the fourth consecutive officer responsible for vault access to die under unexplained circumstances since audits began.

NX
By Nyx Shadowmere Crime & Justice Reporter
23 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

Watch Commander Renna Blackshore was discovered dead in her private study at the Hour of Third Bell yesterday morning, approximately nine hours after receiving a judicial subpoena to testify before Magistrate Thornwood's ongoing treasury investigation. Her death marks the fourth fatality among officers with direct responsibility for Crown Reserve vault security since temple auditors began their count six days ago. The City Watch is calling it a heart seizure. I am calling it what it is: another name on a list that grows longer every time someone gets close to documenting who has been walking in and out of Ardia's treasury for the past forty years.

Blackshore served twenty-three years in the Watch, the last seven as commander of the Palace District precinct — the unit responsible for exterior security of the Crown Reserve complex. Her subpoena, which I obtained from court records this morning, specifically requested testimony regarding "irregularities in overnight access logs" between Year 842 and Year 846 AP. Those years coincide precisely with the period when, according to preliminary audit findings, the bulk of the missing 340,000 sovereigns appears to have vanished. Her predecessor in the Palace District command, Watch Commander Aldric Venn, died in what was ruled a drowning accident eighteen months ago. The commander before him took early retirement to a village outside Valdris and was found dead of apparent natural causes two weeks later. The records clerk who maintained their duty rosters died in her home last Frosthold. No investigation was opened.

I spent yesterday afternoon in the sealed Watch archives that Elara Sunforge's investigation recently revealed — the ones showing uninvestigated deaths among Crown Reserve auditors spanning three decades. Commander Blackshore's name appears in those records, but not as a victim. She signed off on the closure of two separate inquiries into suspicious deaths near the vault complex, citing "insufficient evidence of foul play" in cases where the evidence was never collected because she ordered her officers not to collect it. Whether she was complicit or coerced, I cannot yet determine. What I can determine is that someone ensured she would never answer that question under oath.

The Watch physician who examined Blackshore's body told me, off the record, that heart seizures do not typically cause the kind of facial expression he observed. "Terror," he said. "Absolute terror, frozen in place." He has requested an Arcane Academy examiner review the remains for evidence of magical interference. That request was denied by Acting Watch Captain Beren Ashford — no relation to the late Mirela Ashford — within two hours of being filed. When I asked Captain Ashford why he blocked a routine forensic examination, he declined to comment and suggested I "focus on matters within journalistic purview." I have been covering this city's dead for six years. This is precisely within my purview.

Magistrate Thornwood has added Blackshore's death to her active investigation and issued preservation orders for all Palace District records. Fourteen Watch officers assigned to vault-adjacent duties have requested immediate transfers since yesterday's discovery; seven of those requests have already been approved. Someone is cleaning house before the auditors finish counting, and they are using methods that leave no obvious blade marks. I have begun compiling a list of every person with documented access to Crown Reserve security protocols who is still alive. It is a shorter list than it should be, and I intend to publish it before it gets any shorter.
