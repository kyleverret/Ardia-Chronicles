City Watch Internal Affairs Unit Disbanded After Lead Investigator Found With Obsidian Ring Contract in His Quarters

Documents reveal the unit meant to root out Watch corruption was itself compromised. Three officers have fled Praxis.

NX
By Nyx Shadowmere Crime & Justice Reporter
23 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

The City Watch's Internal Affairs Unit — the division responsible for investigating corruption within Watch ranks — has been dissolved effective immediately after documents recovered from Lead Investigator Caelum Vex's quarters revealed a signed contract with the Obsidian Ring criminal network. Vex himself has not been seen since yesterday morning, and Watch officials confirmed this afternoon that he and two subordinate officers crossed the southern gate before dawn and have not returned. The contract, which I have reviewed through a source within the Magistrate's office, promises Vex 2,000 gold sovereigns annually in exchange for 'advance notice of any investigative interest in Ring operations or personnel.'

The implications extend far beyond one corrupt officer. Internal Affairs has been the primary body reviewing Watch conduct since the corruption purge of Year 841 AP — the same purge that led to seven dismissals and my own departure from the Watch's organized crime unit. Every case that unit cleared, every officer it investigated and exonerated, must now be considered suspect. Magistrate Thornwood has ordered an immediate review of all Internal Affairs findings from the past three years, a process that will require resources the court system can barely spare given the ongoing Crown Reserve investigation.

I contacted Investigator Rael Mourne, my former Watch partner, who confirmed that rank-and-file officers are 'furious and demoralized in equal measure.' Mourne said several junior officers had brought concerns about suspicious case closures to Internal Affairs over the past eighteen months, only to have their complaints dismissed without explanation. 'We thought we were being paranoid,' Mourne told me. 'Turns out we weren't paranoid enough.'

The Obsidian Ring has operated in Praxis for at least two decades, running protection schemes, smuggling operations, and — as recent reporting has suggested — financial fraud networks that may connect to the shell companies under investigation in the Crown Reserve scandal. Their ability to compromise the very unit designed to catch corrupted officers represents a level of institutional penetration that should alarm every citizen who relies on the Watch for protection. High Regent Marcen's office issued a brief statement calling the situation 'deeply troubling' and promising 'a full accounting,' but offered no specifics on how oversight of the Watch will function while a replacement structure is established.

I have spent six years at the Chronicle building sources inside the criminal networks of this city. The Obsidian Ring does not make contracts lightly, and they do not make contracts with people who cannot deliver value. Caelum Vex was not a minor figure — he controlled which investigations went forward and which quietly died. The question we must now ask is not simply how many cases he buried, but who else in positions of authority carries similar contracts in their quarters, waiting to be discovered only after the damage is done.
