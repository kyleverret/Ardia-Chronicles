Obsidian Ring Ledger Found in Disbanded Internal Affairs Office Lists Active Payments to Fourteen City Watch Officers

Document recovered by court-appointed auditors names recipients still serving in Veil District and Harbor Command. Watch Commander position remains vacant.

NX
By Nyx Shadowmere Crime & Justice Reporter
23 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

Court-appointed auditors searching the offices of the disbanded City Watch Internal Affairs Unit have recovered a leather-bound ledger containing what appears to be a complete payment record for Obsidian Ring bribes to active Watch officers—fourteen of whom remain on duty across Praxis as of this morning. The document, discovered in a hidden compartment beneath former Lead Investigator Caelum Vex's desk, lists names, ranks, assigned districts, and monthly payment amounts ranging from fifteen to two hundred gold crowns. I have confirmed the identities of six officers on the list; all six reported for their regular shifts yesterday.

Magistrate Thornwood's office received the ledger at dawn and immediately sealed it under evidence protection. However, three sources with direct knowledge of its contents have independently confirmed the scope of what it reveals. The payments span at least thirty-two months, predating Vex's appointment to lead Internal Affairs. "This was not one corrupt investigator," one source told me. "This was an ongoing business relationship. Vex inherited the operation and expanded it."

The fourteen named officers serve in positions that would grant the Obsidian Ring extraordinary operational advantages. Four work Harbor Command, where smuggled goods enter the city. Three patrol the Veil District, where the Ring maintains its strongest territorial presence. Two serve in the evidence lockup at Central Watch House. The remaining five hold various posts, but each controls access to information or physical spaces that criminal networks would pay dearly to compromise.

High Regent Marcen's office has not responded to requests for comment. The Watch Commander position remains unfilled following Commander Aldric Stonehart's death last week—hours after he received a subpoena to testify on Crown Reserve security protocols. With Internal Affairs disbanded and no commander to order disciplinary action, the fourteen officers named in the ledger face no immediate consequences. They will report for duty again tomorrow.

I spoke this morning with Investigator Rael Mourne, my former Watch partner and one of the few senior investigators who emerged from the corruption review with their reputation intact. When I asked whether honest officers could function in a department this compromised, Mourne was silent for a long moment. "We always knew there were names we didn't have," Mourne finally said. "Now we have them. The question is whether anyone left has the authority to act on them." The Obsidian Ring, for its part, continues to operate openly in three districts. Their lieutenants have been seen conducting business in daylight since the Internal Affairs disbandment. They appear to understand, perhaps better than the Grand Council does, that the institution designed to stop them no longer exists in any meaningful form.
