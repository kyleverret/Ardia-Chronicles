Rangers Recover Vindil's Private Journals From Tower—Pages Describe 'Successful' Resurrection Attempts Dating Back Decades

Encrypted volumes detail experiments on battlefield dead from wars Seldîré officially never fought. Remaining founders have requested the journals be destroyed unread.

NX
By Nyx Shadowmere Crime & Justice Reporter
28 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

THORNHAVEN BORDER REGION — Ranger search teams combing the base of Vindil's tower have recovered what investigators are calling the most disturbing evidence yet of the founding hero's betrayal: seventeen encrypted journals spanning approximately two hundred years, containing detailed accounts of necromantic experiments that the fallen founder described in his own hand as 'successful beyond expectation.' The journals were discovered in a hidden vault beneath the tower's foundation, accessible only through a passage that collapsed during Vindil's final confrontation with Ranger forces.

According to sources within the Ranger corps who spoke on condition of anonymity, the earliest dated entries reference battlefields from conflicts that do not appear in any official Seldîré historical record—wars the elvish kingdom has never publicly acknowledged fighting. 'The bodies he describes collecting weren't from monster attacks or border skirmishes,' one source told the Chronicle. 'He writes about organized battles, military formations, casualties in the hundreds. Either he traveled far beyond Seldîré's borders to find subjects, or the kingdom's history is missing chapters no one alive remembers.'

The six surviving founders issued a joint request through diplomatic channels yesterday asking that the journals be sealed and destroyed without further examination, citing 'the sacred privacy of a companion who has already paid for his crimes' and 'the risk of dangerous knowledge spreading beyond those equipped to contain it.' High Minister Caelindra of Seldîré has not yet responded to the request, though sources close to her office indicate she is consulting with the kingdom's senior archivists about preservation protocols for evidence in ongoing criminal investigations.

Elbreth, the apprentice whose report initiated the investigation that led to Vindil's death or disappearance, has declined all interview requests through her appointed advocate. However, court filings reveal that she provided investigators with a partial cipher key she discovered during her studies—a key that has already allowed preliminary translation of three journal volumes. What those translations contain has not been disclosed, but two Ranger investigators who reviewed the material have requested reassignment to other duties, and the Head Minister's office has summoned a specialist in traumatic testimony from the Arcane Academy's Department of Incorporeal Studies.

The question that haunts Thornhaven's border community tonight is not merely what Vindil did in his tower for two centuries, but what the other founders knew and when they knew it. 'Six heroes don't share a kingdom with a seventh for eight hundred years without noticing something wrong,' observed retired Watch Captain Mira Ashdale, who served thirty years on the Thornhaven border patrol. 'Either they knew and did nothing, or they deliberately chose not to look. I'm not sure which answer is worse.' The remaining founders have not responded to requests for clarification about their knowledge of Vindil's activities, and their joint statement continues to emphasize grief rather than accountability. The journals, for now, remain in Ranger custody—unread, undestroyed, and waiting.
