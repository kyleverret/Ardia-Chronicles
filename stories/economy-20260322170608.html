Crown Reserve Vaults Opened for First Independent Audit in Forty Years; Initial Count Shows Massive Discrepancy

Temple auditors report 340,000 gold sovereigns missing from official tallies. Banking Guild demands immediate Council explanation.

ES
By Elara Sunforge Business & Economy Editor
22 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

The Crown Reserve Vaults opened their doors yesterday morning for the first time since Year 807 AP — not to a Council-appointed committee, but to independent auditors from the Temple of the Radiant Dawn operating under Magistrate Thornwood's emergency jurisdiction. By sundown, those auditors had counted the contents of the first three chambers and found them short by approximately 340,000 gold sovereigns against official treasury ledgers. That sum represents roughly eight percent of the Crown's stated liquid reserves.

The audit exists because of the late Councilor Maren Ashworth, whose emergency request triggered her disappearance three weeks ago. Magistrate Thornwood invoked an obscure provision of the Treasury Accountability Act of Year 762 that permits judicial override when "reasonable suspicion of material fraud affecting Crown solvency" can be demonstrated. The fourteen Council members I identified last week as recipients of payments from Ardian Futures Holdings attempted to block the order; they were overruled by a coalition of Reform members and three Expansionists who apparently decided protecting Lord Commander Drath's military budget outweighed protecting their colleagues.

"The discrepancy is not a counting error," said Temple Auditor Seraphina Coldwell, who led the examination team. "The vault records show deposits that never occurred and withdrawals attributed to projects we can find no evidence ever existed. Someone has been systematically falsifying Crown accounts for years." When I asked how many years, Coldwell declined to speculate until her team completes its review of the remaining twelve chambers — a process she estimates will take another week.

The missing funds would explain much that has puzzled me over the past month. The frantic efforts to prevent Ashworth's audit. The payments to Council members overseeing treasury oversight. The sudden freeze on magitech firm accounts that were, I now suspect, being used to launder sovereign gold into private hands. The crystal stockpiling may have been less about market manipulation than about converting Crown assets into portable, untraceable wealth before the inevitable reckoning arrived.

High Regent Marcen issued a brief statement acknowledging the audit's preliminary findings and promising "full cooperation with judicial authorities." More telling was what she did not say: she did not defend the treasury officials whose signatures appear on the falsified records, and she did not name Councilor Aldric Pemberton when listing those she expects to assist the investigation. Pemberton, who refused the third signature on Ashworth's audit request, has not been seen at Council chambers since yesterday's count began. His office claims he is "reviewing documents at an undisclosed location." I have heard that phrase before. It rarely precedes a satisfactory explanation.

I will be at the vaults when they open Chamber Four this morning. Somewhere in those records lies the answer to a question I have been asking since Councilor Ashworth vanished: who has been stealing from Ardia's treasury, and what have they been buying with our gold? The crystal markets, the frozen magitech firms, the secret payments — they are all threads of the same rope. Someone has been preparing for something expensive, and they have been funding it with money that belonged to every citizen of this realm. I intend to find out what.
