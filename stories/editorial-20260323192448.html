The Dead Do Not Serve

When soldiers officially killed in action appear fighting for our enemies, the Grand Council owes us more than silence.

?
By aldric-voss
23 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

I have written obituaries for soldiers. I have approved headlines announcing casualties from the Shadow Frontier. I have, in my capacity as editor of this paper, trusted the official records of the Grand Army when they reported who lived and who died in service to this realm. That trust is now in question. Field medics have confirmed that wounded Shadow Legion fighters carry the unit tattoos of the 7th Eastern Regiment—soldiers whose families received death notifications three years ago.

There are limited explanations for this evidence, and none of them are comfortable. Either the Grand Army falsified casualty records to conceal desertions to the enemy, or someone with access to military documentation is forging unit markings on Shadow Legion operatives to manufacture exactly this kind of confusion, or—and this possibility demands consideration—soldiers we buried as heroes were subsequently delivered, alive, to forces we have spent three years fighting. Each explanation implicates institutions we depend upon for security and truth.

The Grand Council cannot meet this revelation with silence. High Regent Marcen has invoked emergency authority to suspend committees investigating other matters; she can certainly find the authority to demand answers about dead soldiers who appear to have returned to life wearing enemy colors. The families of the 7th Eastern Regiment deserve to know whether they buried their children or buried lies. The citizens of Ardia deserve to know whether our military records mean anything at all.

This paper reported the 7th Eastern's losses when they occurred. If those reports were built on false foundations, we share in the failure—not of intent, but of verification. We trusted official sources. We will not make that mistake again without demanding proof. The dead do not serve. If they are serving, they were never dead. And if they were never dead, someone in the Grand Army signed documents saying otherwise. Find that person. The Chronicle will be watching.
