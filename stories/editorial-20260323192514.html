The Dead Do Not Collect Military Pay

When soldiers officially killed three years ago appear alive on enemy lines, someone signed the paperwork that made them disappear.

?
By cassian-webb
23 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

I have edited thousands of obituaries in my years at this desk. The grammar of death is simple: a name, a date, a cause. What I cannot edit is a lie dressed as a death certificate. Field medics at the Shadow Frontier have now confirmed what rumor has whispered for months—wounded Shadow Legion fighters bear the unit tattoos of the Grand Army's 7th Eastern Regiment, men and women whose official records declare them killed in action three years ago. Someone wrote those records. Someone signed them. Someone collected the death benefits while the soldiers themselves marched under a different banner.

The passive voice is the coward's refuge, so let me be direct: military death certificates require signatures from commanding officers, regimental clerks, and the Grand Army's Records Division in Praxis. A single fraudulent document might escape notice. Dozens of soldiers from a single disbanded regiment, all falsely declared dead, all somehow appearing in Shadow Legion ranks? That is not clerical error. That is either spectacular incompetence or deliberate orchestration, and I have not survived six decades of bureaucratic prose by believing in coincidence.

Lord Commander Severin Drath has spent months demanding expanded military authority through the Shadow Frontier Authorization Act. He argues the Legion represents an existential threat requiring extraordinary powers. Perhaps he is right. But before we grant any commander extraordinary authority, we deserve to know how soldiers under Grand Council command ended up carrying Shadow Legion weapons—and who signed the documents that made their transformation invisible. The dead do not collect military pay. Someone living does.

High Regent Marcen has suspended three Council committees this week alone. If she wishes to demonstrate that emergency authority serves the realm rather than shields it from scrutiny, she could begin by ordering the Records Division to produce every signature on every death certificate from the 7th Eastern Regiment. Names, dates, chain of custody. The grammar of accountability is also simple: who knew, when, and what did they authorize? We have waited long enough for answers written in something other than vanishing ink.
