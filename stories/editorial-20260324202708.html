The Guild That Polices Itself Has Chosen to Police No One

When sabotage components are manufactured inside Artificers' Guild headquarters, self-regulation has failed catastrophically.

?
By seraphina-cole
24 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

I have defended guild self-governance in this space before. I have argued that craftspeople who understand their trades make better regulators than distant bureaucrats, that the Artificers' Guild's centuries of institutional knowledge justify its privileged position in Ardia's licensing framework. I was wrong, and the evidence of my error carries microscopic maker's marks traceable to restricted fabrication equipment inside Guild headquarters itself.

Let us be precise about what this means: the kill-switch components designed to destroy automatons and corrupt the ley-line taps powering southeastern Praxis were not smuggled past Guild inspectors. They were manufactured by Guild artificers, using Guild equipment, in Guild facilities. Three senior artificers have fled the city rather than answer questions. The organization entrusted with protecting the public from dangerous magitech was, at minimum, the source of that danger—and we are asked to believe this happened without institutional knowledge or complicity.

The Grand Council granted the Artificers' Guild self-regulatory authority in Year 812 AP because lawmakers believed no external body could match the Guild's technical expertise. That argument sounds hollow when Guild expertise was apparently deployed to build sabotage devices capable of cascading infrastructure collapse. We do not let the Alchemists' Consortium investigate its own poisonings. We should not let the Artificers' Guild investigate its own sabotage. High Regent Marcen must appoint an independent commission with subpoena authority—one that includes Kira Ashford, whose unauthorized automaton has already demonstrated better diagnostic capabilities than certified Guild inspectors ever managed.

The citizens of Praxis entrusted their safety to institutions that promised accountability. Those institutions manufactured the threat instead. We owe it to every family who might have lost power, lost livelihoods, lost lives to demand something better than guild seals stamped on guild crimes.
