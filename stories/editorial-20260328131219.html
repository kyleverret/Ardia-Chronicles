When the Evidence Burns, the Arsonist Wears a Guild Seal

The Artificers' Guild records archive fire is not a coincidence, and treating it as one insults every citizen of Praxis.

?
By seraphina-cole
28 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

I wrote three days ago that the Artificers' Guild could not be trusted to investigate itself. I did not imagine they would prove my point by burning down the evidence before anyone else could examine it. The Guild claims an electrical fault destroyed the basement archive—the same basement where fabrication equipment produced forty-three kill-switch components, the same archive where activation logs would have shown exactly which senior artificers authorized the production of devices designed to cripple Praxis. We are asked to believe this timing is coincidental. We are asked to accept that an organization sophisticated enough to manufacture sabotage circuits somehow cannot prevent an electrical fire in its own headquarters.

I do not believe it, and neither should you. Three artificers with basement access fled the city rather than answer questions. Now the records that would have implicated them—and whoever gave them their orders—are ash. The Collective, that network of independent artificers who have been sounding alarms about Guild corruption for years, anticipated exactly this. They released Seven's diagnostic protocols to every workshop in Praxis before dawn, ensuring that at least some proof survives in distributed form. An unauthorized automaton showed more commitment to accountability than the institution chartered to protect us.

High Regent Marcen's emergency powers expire at midnight. When the sun rises tomorrow, suspended Council committees can resume their investigations—into Crown Reserve withdrawals, into the connections between Ardian Futures Holdings and Guild leadership, into all the threads that someone is desperately trying to burn before they can be followed. I am calling on every councillor who cares about the integrity of our institutions to demand that the Guild fire investigation be transferred immediately to an independent body with real subpoena authority. Kira Ashford and Seven have already demonstrated what genuine investigation looks like. It is long past time our official institutions learned from their example.

The architects of this conspiracy are watching us. They are counting on our institutions to fail, on our attention to wander, on the ashes to cool before we ask who lit the match. We owe it to the families who nearly lost power, to the automatons marked for destruction, to every citizen who trusted a Guild seal to mean something—we owe them answers that cannot be burned away.
