Forest Spirit Elders Break Centuries of Silence to Warn Grand Council of 'Wound Beneath the World'

Three ancient guardians manifest at Council chambers demanding audience. Their testimony names the Ironvein Mining Consortium as the Greyward's source.

VM
By Vael Moonstrike Environment & Nature Correspondent
22 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

PRAXIS — For the first time in four hundred and seventeen years, forest spirits have crossed the boundaries of their groves to address mortal governance directly. Three elder guardians — beings whose names translate roughly as Rootdeep, Mosswhisper, and She-Who-Holds-Still-Water — manifested within the Grand Council chambers yesterday at the Hour of Long Shadows, their forms flickering between visibility and something my eyes could not quite hold. I was present in the gallery when they arrived. The temperature dropped fifteen degrees in an instant, and every piece of living wood in the chamber — chairs, podiums, the ancient oak beams overhead — began to hum with a frequency I felt in my teeth.

The spirits spoke in the Old Tongue, which only three Council members could understand without translation. I have carried that language since childhood, taught by my grandmother in the border forests, and I will record their words exactly: "Something drinks the blood of the world beneath your stone cities. It has a name you know. It pays your merchants gold. It sits in seats like these." When Chancellor Morcroft demanded clarification, Rootdeep — the largest of the three, whose form suggested an oak that had grown for a thousand years — named the Ironvein Mining Consortium directly. The spirits claim that Ironvein's deep excavations in the Thornback Mountains have breached what they call the Wellspring Channels — underground ley-currents that feed magical resonance to every living system in eastern Ardia.

I have spent six months documenting the Greyward's spread: the dying resonance in Miravel Woods, the barren spawning grounds of the River Veyla, the silenced stones of Keldara. The spirits confirmed what I had feared but could not prove — these are not separate catastrophes but symptoms of a single wound. "Your miners have cut the roots that feed the forest, the river, the stone," Mosswhisper said, her voice like wind through autumn leaves. "Every day they dig deeper. Every day more of what we protect goes quiet." She-Who-Holds-Still-Water added that three more groves have fallen silent in the past week alone, in locations I had not yet reached.

The Ironvein Mining Consortium has operated under a royal charter for thirty-one years, extracting iron, silver, and rare magical ores from the Thornback range. Their largest investor, according to Banking Guild records I obtained this morning, is the Ardian Heritage Foundation — the same opaque entity that Elara Sunforge connected to the Crown Reserve discrepancies before Councilor Ashworth's disappearance. When I attempted to reach Ironvein's chairman, Lord Castor Velmeer, his office issued a statement calling the spirits' testimony "the superstitious imaginings of trees" and questioning whether forest entities possess legal standing to address the Council at all.

That question may define what comes next. Magistrate Thornwood — the same jurist currently sheltering human witnesses under temple sanctuary — has accepted a petition to hear the spirits' testimony under an ancient precedent I had to dig through Archive records to find. The Covenant of Living Voices, signed during the reign of Queen Aelindra the Wise, grants standing to "any being of the natural world whose existence is threatened by mortal action." The last time it was invoked, in Year 430, a river spirit's testimony shut down a tannery that was poisoning three villages downstream. Lord Velmeer's attorneys have already filed to block the hearing.

I stood in the Council chamber for an hour after the spirits departed, watching the oak beams slowly stop humming. Rootdeep's final words remain with me: "We do not come to your halls because we wish to. We come because we are dying, and you are the ones holding the knife." The Greyward is spreading. The spirits have named its source. Now the question is whether Ardia's institutions will listen to voices that have guarded this land since before we built our first cities — or whether we will let them fall silent too, because their testimony is inconvenient for powerful interests. I will be at Magistrate Thornwood's hearing. I will record every word. And I will keep writing until someone with the authority to stop this chooses to act.
