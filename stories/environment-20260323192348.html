Forest Spirit Refugees Arrive at Thornhaven Gates Seeking Sanctuary From 'The Silence That Eats'

First recorded mass displacement of incorporeal beings in Ardian history. Border officials struggle to apply Treaty of Thornhaven to non-physical entities.

VM
By Vael Moonstrike Environment & Nature Correspondent
23 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

THORNHAVEN — I arrived at the western gates before dawn yesterday, summoned by a Forest Warden Guild emergency dispatch that said only: "They are coming out of the trees." What I found there defies easy description. Seventeen forest spirits — beings of light and memory who have inhabited the Thornwood groves since before humans built their first settlement in Ardia — had gathered at the boundary stones, their forms dim and flickering in ways I have never witnessed in seven years of communion with their kind. They were asking to enter the city.

Forest spirits do not leave their groves. This is not preference or tradition — it is fundamental to their nature, or so the Arcane Academy's classification texts have taught for centuries. The spirits exist in symbiosis with specific trees, specific streams, specific patches of earth. Displacement should be impossible. Yet here they stood, incorporeal figures whose light pulsed with what my warden training recognized as distress, requesting something that our laws have no framework to address: refuge from their own homeland.

"The Silence comes behind us," said the spirit who identified herself through the old communion tongue as Whisper-of-Morning-Rain. I knelt at the boundary stones to receive her words, my warden's cord warm against my skin for the first time since the ley-line nexus went dark. "Where Silence passes, we cease. Not death — there is no death for us as your kind knows it. We simply stop being. The memories we hold, the songs of ten thousand seasons, the names of every creature who sheltered beneath our canopies — all of it, unmade." When I asked what caused this Silence, she flickered so violently I feared she would dissipate entirely. "It rises from beneath," she said. "From the wounds your miners have opened. From the emptiness they feed."

Thornhaven's co-administrators — Mayor Corvin Ashdale and Faelindis of the Western Grove — convened an emergency session before noon to address what Ashdale called "a situation that exists nowhere in our legal codes." The Treaty of Thornhaven grants passage rights and citizenship pathways to elven and human refugees, but its framers in Year 801 AP apparently never contemplated that the forest itself might need to flee. "We cannot refuse beings who have watched over these lands since before our ancestors learned to forge iron," Faelindis told the hastily assembled council. "But we also cannot grant legal status to entities who possess no physical form, own no property, and exist outside every system of documentation we have built."

I have submitted urgent inquiries to the Grand Council's Land and Resources Committee and to the Arcane Academy's Department of Incorporeal Studies, requesting guidance on how Ardia intends to respond to what is now clearly a crisis extending far beyond economics or politics. The Greyward Anomaly, the walking Mother-Trees, the collapse of the Thornwood ley-line nexus — these were warnings. The spirits fleeing their ancestral groves are something else entirely: evidence that whatever is happening beneath our realm is not merely disrupting the natural order, but consuming it. As of this morning, four more spirits have arrived at the gates. Whisper-of-Morning-Rain says there are hundreds more behind them, moving slower, their light fading as the Silence spreads. I will remain in Thornhaven to document their testimony, however long that takes. Someone must remember what they carry, in case there comes a day when they no longer can.
