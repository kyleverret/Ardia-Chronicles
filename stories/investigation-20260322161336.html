Crown Physician's Personal Secretary Found Dead Hours Before Scheduled Testimony to Magistrate Thornwood

Lyra Ashford, 34, discovered in her locked apartment with signs of shadowbind venom. She held the keys to Dr. Aldric Vane's private appointment ledgers.

SN
By Sera Nightwhisper Investigative Journalist
22 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

Lyra Ashford kept meticulous records. For eleven years, she managed Crown Physician Aldric Vane's private schedule — the appointments that never appeared in official palace logs, the consultations conducted in estate libraries rather than examination rooms, the payments received in trade goods rather than coin. She was scheduled to deliver those records to Magistrate Elena Thornwood at nine o'clock this morning. At seven-fifteen, her landlord found her body on the floor of her sitting room. The door was locked from the inside. The windows were sealed. Her appointment ledgers were gone.

I arrived at the Thornwell District address before the Watch had finished securing the scene. The temple healer conducting the preliminary examination — Brother Aldous Mere, the same healer who identified shadowbind traces on the guard killed at Ironhold Keep — confirmed the cause of death within minutes. "Same compound," he told me, his voice flat with exhaustion. "Same paralytic signature. She would have been conscious but unable to move or call for help. Death would have taken approximately four hours." Four hours. Lyra Ashford lay dying while someone searched her home, removed every document connecting Dr. Vane to his private patients, and departed through means the Watch has not yet explained.

My investigation into the Crown Physician's activities over the past three weeks has established connections between his private practice and at least seven deaths among noble families — deaths officially attributed to natural causes, all involving patients who had recently challenged guild reform legislation or threatened to expose financial irregularities in crown-adjacent institutions. Ashford's testimony was to provide the documentary evidence linking those deaths to specific appointments, specific payments, and specific compounds procured through channels I have traced to the same smuggling networks once operated by House Vanthorn. Without her ledgers, that chain of evidence has been severed.

Magistrate Thornwood, who has now lost five potential witnesses in the expanding corruption investigation, issued a statement from the Temple of the Radiant Dawn, where her three remaining witnesses shelter under the Sanctuary Covenant. "Someone is erasing the truth one life at a time," she said. "Lyra Ashford was a secretary who wrote things down. That was her only crime." When I asked Watch Commander Aldric Venn whether the investigation into Ashford's death would be assigned to Captain Sera Blackwood — who has now handled four connected cases and produced no arrests — he terminated our conversation without response.

I have spent fourteen years at this newspaper learning to follow evidence rather than speculation. The evidence now points in a direction I approach with full awareness of its implications. Crown Physician Aldric Vane has served three successive monarchs. He has treated every member of the royal family for two decades. His signature appears on death certificates that closed investigations into the very nobles whose reform efforts threatened entrenched interests. And every person who could testify to his private activities is now dead, missing, or hiding behind sacred wards. I requested an interview with Dr. Vane through official palace channels this morning. I was informed he is "unavailable for the foreseeable future" and that further inquiries should be directed to the Crown's legal counsel. I have noted the response. I have not ceased my inquiries.

Lyra Ashford left behind a sister in the Riverside District and a small grey cat that was found beside her body, refusing to leave. The sister, when I spoke with her briefly, asked me only one question: "Will anyone remember that she tried to do the right thing?" I told her I would make certain of it. The records Ashford kept may be gone, but the appointments happened. The patients existed. The payments were made. Somewhere in Praxis, there are other people who know what the Crown Physician did behind closed doors — people who have not yet been found by whoever is conducting this systematic elimination of witnesses. I intend to reach them first.
