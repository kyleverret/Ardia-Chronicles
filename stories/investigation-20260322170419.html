Sealed Watch Archives Reveal Pattern of Uninvestigated Deaths Among Crown Reserve Auditors

Documents obtained by the Chronicle show seventeen financial examiners died under suspicious circumstances over two decades. None received full investigation.

SN
By Sera Nightwhisper Investigative Journalist
22 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

Seventeen auditors assigned to examine the Crown Reserve Vaults have died under circumstances officially classified as accidental or natural since Year 825 AP. Not one of these deaths received a complete City Watch investigation. I obtained records confirming this pattern through sources I will not identify, and I have spent the past three weeks verifying each case against publicly available death registries, Watch incident logs, and employment records from the Treasury Division.

The pattern is unmistakable. Auditor Callum Thorne drowned in four inches of bathwater in Year 831 AP — ruled accidental. Senior Examiner Petra Vance fell from a second-floor window in Year 838 AP — no witnesses, case closed in nine days. Deputy Comptroller Marcus Ashwell suffered "acute magical exhaustion" in Year 844 AP while reviewing vault access logs — the same diagnosis recently applied to court clerk Mirela Ashford, who died in a sealed archive room connected to the Vanthorn prosecution. Each of these individuals had recently requested expanded access to vault inventory records. Each died before completing their assigned reviews.

I presented this evidence to Watch Inspector Fenra Cales, who confirmed that her department's involvement in these cases was limited by jurisdictional claims from what she called "higher offices." She would not specify which offices. She did note that formal requests for case files on six of these deaths have been denied under provisions of the Crown Security Act — a statute typically reserved for matters of military intelligence. "I cannot tell you what I do not have access to know," Inspector Cales said. "I can tell you that seventeen is not a coincidence."

The Crown Reserve Vaults — the same facilities that vanished Councilor Maren Ashworth was investigating before her disappearance — have not undergone a complete independent audit since Year 822 AP. Every auditor assigned to conduct one has either died, resigned citing personal reasons, or been reassigned to different duties. The Treasury Division declined to comment on personnel matters. The High Regent's office referred my questions to the Grand Council's finance committee, whose chair, Councilor Aldric Pemberton, is among the fourteen officials identified in Elara Sunforge's recent reporting as receiving unexplained payments from entities connected to the crystal stockpiling scandal.

I do not yet know what those vaults contain that justifies two decades of dead auditors and sealed case files. I do know that Councilor Ashworth was asking the same questions when she vanished, that Mirela Ashford died with documents that might have answered them, and that someone with considerable authority has worked very hard to ensure those answers stay buried. I will continue this investigation. The dead deserve that much. So does everyone who trusts that our institutions serve the public rather than those with the power to make inconvenient people disappear.
