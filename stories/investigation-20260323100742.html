Crown Physician's Financial Records Show Payments Routed Through Same Shell Company as Missing Reserve Funds

Temple auditors tracing 340,000 missing sovereigns discover Ardian Futures Holdings also processed fees for Dr. Aldric Vanthorn's 'private consultations.'

SN
By Sera Nightwhisper Investigative Journalist
23 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

The investigation into the Crown Reserve discrepancy has intersected with my ongoing examination of deaths connected to Crown Physician Aldric Vanthorn. Temple auditors reviewing frozen accounts at Ardian Futures Holdings have identified seventeen payments totaling 12,400 gold sovereigns processed through the same subsidiary that handled secret disbursements to Council members — payments that correspond precisely with dates I had previously documented as "private consultations" in Dr. Vanthorn's seized practice records.

I have spent fourteen months building a case against the Crown Physician. The pattern was clear: three noble deaths linked to an untraceable compound, a personal secretary killed before testimony, sealed Watch archives showing uninvestigated deaths among officials who questioned Crown finances. What I could not establish was the connection between a court physician's poisoning operation and the broader financial conspiracy that has now exposed a 340,000-sovereign hole in Ardia's treasury. The temple auditors found it in a single afternoon.

Ardian Futures Holdings processed payments to Dr. Vanthorn on six occasions between Year 844 and Year 846 AP. Each payment occurred within two weeks of a death I had flagged in my investigation — including Lord Cedric Ashworth, the Crown Reserve auditor whose files later revealed discrepancies that his successor was instructed not to pursue. The payments were logged as "consulting fees for specialized medical services." There is no record of what services were rendered or to whom.

I presented these findings to Magistrate Thornwood yesterday evening. She has issued a summons compelling Dr. Vanthorn to appear before her court within seventy-two hours. His residence in the Crown Quarter was found empty this morning; household staff claim he departed for "a medical conference in Valdris" three days ago. The Valdris Medical Society confirms no such conference exists. City Watch Inspector Fenra Cales has posted observers at all major roads leaving the capital.

The fourteen Council members who received payments from Ardian Futures Holdings include three who sat on the committee that approved Dr. Vanthorn's appointment as Crown Physician in Year 842 AP. Their votes were unanimous. The previous Crown Physician, Dr. Helena Ashford, had resigned abruptly after requesting an independent audit of royal household medical expenditures — a request that was denied. Dr. Ashford died of an apparent illness seven months later. Her death was not investigated. I am requesting her medical records this morning.

Someone has been killing the people who asked questions about Ardia's finances. That same someone has been paying the Crown's own physician through channels now linked to the largest theft in the realm's history. The connection is no longer theoretical. I have names, dates, and amounts. What remains is to establish who authorized these payments — and whether the person treating the High Regent's own household has been operating as an assassin with a royal appointment. I will have answers before Dr. Vanthorn reaches whatever border he is running toward.
