Temple Truth-Reader Who Verified Crown Reserve Audits Found Dead in Locked Meditation Chamber

Brother Aldric Fenmore was the sole temple official with clearance to authenticate withdrawal signatures. His death leaves no living witness to the verification process.

SN
By Sera Nightwhisper Investigative Journalist
23 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

Brother Aldric Fenmore, the senior truth-reader who authenticated every signature verification request from the Crown Reserve Vaults for the past eleven years, was found dead yesterday morning in the Temple of the Scales' inner sanctum — a chamber accessible only through three separately warded doors, each requiring different temple authorities to unseal. I confirmed his identity through two independent sources within the temple hierarchy who spoke on condition of anonymity due to the sensitivity of the ongoing treasury investigation.

Fenmore, 67, was discovered by junior acolytes who had grown concerned when he failed to emerge for morning devotions. The chamber's wards showed no evidence of tampering. His body bore no visible wounds. Temple Magistrate Corin Ashwood has ordered the chamber sealed pending a full arcane forensic examination, but preliminary reports from temple healers indicate his heart simply stopped — the same cause of death recorded for Crown Physician Aldric Vane's personal secretary, who died hours before her scheduled testimony last week.

My sources confirm that Fenmore had been cooperating with Magistrate Thornwood's investigation into the forged withdrawal authorizations discovered on Banking Guild Compliance Chief Theron Ashford's documents. He was the only temple official with both the clearance level and the specific training to verify whether truth-reading protocols had been properly followed during the original signature authentications — protocols that should have detected any forgery at the time of the withdrawal.

I obtained access to temple scheduling records through a source who has proven reliable across three previous investigations. Those records show Fenmore had requested a private audience with High Regent Marcen scheduled for tomorrow morning. The subject of that meeting was listed only as 'verification discrepancies requiring immediate Council attention.' That audience will now never occur.

With Fenmore's death, the chain of witness linking the Crown Reserve's physical vault procedures to the temple's authentication records has been severed entirely. Watch Commander Blackshore, who oversaw vault security, is dead. The Crown Physician's secretary, who maintained his private payment ledgers, is dead. Now the man who could have testified whether the original verifications were genuine or compromised has joined them. Someone is systematically eliminating everyone capable of explaining how 340,000 gold sovereigns vanished from accounts that multiple institutions swore were properly safeguarded.

I asked Temple Magistrate Ashwood whether he had arranged protection for other temple officials involved in the treasury investigation. He declined to answer directly, but his hands were shaking when he sealed the interview chamber behind me.
