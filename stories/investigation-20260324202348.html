Crown Physician's Arrest Warrant Sealed by Magistrate Hours Before City Watch Could Execute It

Source confirms intervention came from Grand Council chamber. Physician's last known location: private dock on Goldrun River.

SN
By Sera Nightwhisper Investigative Journalist
24 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

The City Watch arrived at Crown Physician Aldric Vennhollow's Merchant Quarter residence at the third bell of morning on 24 Thawbreak, carrying an arrest warrant signed by Magistrate Elara Thornwood. They found the door unlocked, the study emptied of documents, and a servant who reported that her employer had departed by private carriage two hours earlier. What the Watch officers did not know—what I have now confirmed through a source inside the Magistrate's office—is that their warrant had been legally voided before they ever left their precinct house.

At some point between Magistrate Thornwood's signature and the Watch's arrival, someone with access to the Grand Council's sealed judicial protocols invoked an emergency stay. The mechanism used is one I have encountered only twice in fourteen years of investigative work: a provision originally created to protect diplomats from foreign realms during sensitive negotiations. It requires authorization from the High Regent's office or a majority vote of the Council's Justice Committee. The Justice Committee did not meet last night. I have submitted formal inquiries to High Regent Marcen's office and received no response.

Crown Physician Vennhollow's private laboratory, seized three days ago, contained seventeen preserved organs from patients whose deaths were officially attributed to natural causes. His financial records, which I obtained through sources I will not identify, show payments routed through the same shell company network now implicated in the Crown Reserve scandal and the counterfeit power cell operation. His personal secretary, Mira Ashvale, was found dead hours before she was scheduled to provide testimony to Magistrate Thornwood. The pattern is not subtle.

Watch Inspector Fenra Cales, who led the attempted arrest, confirmed that her officers traced Vennhollow's carriage to a private dock on the Goldrun River's southern bank. The mooring ropes had been cut. A vessel matching the description of a Shipping Guild fast-courier was observed departing downriver toward Valdris approximately ninety minutes before the Watch arrived. When I asked Cales whether she had jurisdiction to pursue, she was silent for a long moment before answering: 'My jurisdiction ends where someone with more authority decides it ends.'

I have spent fourteen months building this investigation. I have documents linking the Crown Physician to at least three noble deaths, to systematic silencing of witnesses, and to financial transactions that connect his office to the broader network of corruption bleeding this realm's treasury dry. Someone in the Grand Council chambers decided that those connections should not be tested in a magistrate's court. The question I will now pursue is simple: whose signature sealed that warrant, and what do they fear Aldric Vennhollow might say under oath?
