Magistrate Who Sealed Crown Physician's Warrant Found Dead With Same Compound in His Blood

Magistrate Thornwood's death marks fourth fatality connected to Crown Physician investigation in eleven days. City Watch source confirms toxicology match.

SN
By Sera Nightwhisper Investigative Journalist
28 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

Magistrate Aldric Thornwood, who sealed the arrest warrant against Crown Physician Aldric Morcen seventy-two hours ago, was discovered dead in his private chambers early this morning. A City Watch source with direct knowledge of the investigation confirmed to the Chronicle that preliminary toxicology testing detected the same untraceable compound found in the three noble families whose deaths I first documented on 21 Thawbreak—and in the seventeen preserved organs recovered from the Crown Physician's laboratory.

Magistrate Thornwood, 58, was found by his clerk when he failed to appear for morning session. The door to his chambers was locked from the inside. There were no signs of forced entry and no evidence of struggle. A half-finished cup of tea sat on his desk beside unsigned documents that, according to a second source within the Magistrate's Office who requested anonymity, would have unsealed the arrest warrant and authorized its immediate execution. He died, it appears, within hours of reversing his own decision.

This marks the fourth death directly connected to the Crown Physician investigation since I began publishing evidence of systematic poisoning among Guild reform advocates and Crown Reserve auditors. Crown Physician Morcen's personal secretary died hours before scheduled testimony. A Temple truth-reader who verified Reserve audits was found in a locked meditation chamber. Now the magistrate who temporarily shielded Morcen from arrest has fallen to what appears to be the same method his protection was designed to conceal.

I requested comment from the Grand Council's Justice Oversight Committee. Councillor Ashby's office confirmed receipt of my inquiry but provided no statement. High Regent Marcen's office declined to comment on whether the case would be transferred to Council-level jurisdiction given the pattern of witness deaths. City Watch Inspector Fenra Cales, who has worked the Crown Physician case since my initial evidence submission, would say only that her investigators are treating Thornwood's death as connected to the ongoing investigation.

Crown Physician Morcen remains at liberty. The warrant authorizing his arrest remains sealed. The magistrate who sealed it is dead of the poison that warrant would have helped prove he manufactured. I have confirmed with Watch sources that no other magistrate has yet agreed to review the case file—three have formally recused themselves since this morning, citing conflicts of interest they did not specify.

Someone is killing the people who could put Aldric Morcen in chains. The list of those people grows shorter with each edition I file. My investigation continues.
