Chancellor Morcroft's Chief of Staff Vanishes Hours Before Scheduled Testimony on Reserve Withdrawals

Aldric Vance was expected to testify under temple truth-reading protocols. His residence shows no signs of struggle.

LS
By Lyra Stormweave Senior Political Correspondent
23 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

Aldric Vance, Chancellor Vayne Morcroft's chief of staff for the past six years and one of the most powerful unelected officials in Ardia, failed to appear for his scheduled testimony before Magistrate Thornwood's investigative tribunal yesterday morning. When temple guards arrived at his residence in the Praxis diplomatic quarter to escort him to chambers, they found his doors unlocked, his breakfast untouched, and no indication of where he had gone. He has not been seen since.

Vance was scheduled to testify under temple truth-reading protocols regarding his knowledge of withdrawals from the Crown Reserve Vaults — withdrawals that my sources within the Quorum confirm bore authorization stamps from the Chancellor's own office. I have spent three weeks attempting to secure an interview with Vance about these documents. His responses, delivered through intermediaries, grew increasingly terse until they stopped entirely four days ago. Now I understand why.

The timing of his disappearance cannot be coincidence. Four Crown Reserve security officials have died in the past fortnight. Compliance Chief Theron Ashford resigned after discovering forged signatures bearing his name. The shell company Ardian Futures Holdings connects missing treasury funds to payments reaching fourteen Council members. Vance sat at the intersection of all these threads — the man who scheduled the Chancellor's meetings, managed his correspondence, and controlled which documents reached his desk and which quietly vanished into filing cabinets.

Chancellor Morcroft issued a brief statement through his press secretary expressing 'concern for Aldric's wellbeing' and urging anyone with information to contact the City Watch. When I asked whether the Chancellor had spoken with Vance in the days before his disappearance, the press secretary ended our conversation. I have confirmed through Council sources that Morcroft has requested an emergency meeting with High Regent Marcen for later today — a meeting from which all staff have been excluded.

I have covered Chancellor Morcroft since his first term on the Council. I have watched him survive the no-confidence vote by a single ballot, navigate the Vanthorn negotiations, and maintain his coalition through crises that would have destroyed lesser politicians. But coalitions require loyalty, and loyalty requires trust. Aldric Vance knew where every secret was buried. His silence, whether purchased or permanent, suggests that someone decided the cost of his testimony was too high to pay. The question now is whether he fled, was taken, or met the same fate as Watch Commander Blackshore. And the question after that is what he knew that made him worth silencing.
