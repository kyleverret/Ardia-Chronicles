Grand Council Archives Reveal Chancellor Morcroft Signed Secret Immunity Agreement With House Vanthorn Before Exile Order

Document predates public exile decree by eleven days. Three Council members demand Morcroft's immediate resignation.

LS
By Lyra Stormweave Senior Political Correspondent
24 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

Chancellor Vayne Morcroft signed a secret immunity agreement with House Vanthorn eleven days before the Grand Council publicly voted to exile the disgraced noble family, according to documents retrieved yesterday from the Council's sealed archive. The agreement, which bears both Morcroft's personal seal and what archival experts have confirmed is his authentic signature, guaranteed that no member of House Vanthorn would face criminal prosecution for financial crimes 'past, present, or reasonably anticipated' in exchange for their voluntary departure from Ardia. Three Council members who reviewed the document have called for Morcroft's immediate resignation.

I obtained access to the immunity agreement through Archivist Donal Fray, who confirmed that the document had been filed under a classification seal typically reserved for matters of realm security rather than domestic political arrangements. When I asked why a negotiated exile would warrant such protection, Fray declined to speculate but noted that the filing designation was changed twice in the weeks following House Vanthorn's departure—first elevated from standard Council records, then elevated again to the highest restriction level available to non-military documents. 'Someone wanted this buried,' Fray said. 'They used every procedural tool available to ensure it stayed buried.'

The timing of the immunity agreement raises immediate questions about the no-confidence vote that Morcroft survived by a single ballot on 22 Thawbreak. Councillor Maren Ashby of Valdris, who leads the Reform Coalition, told me yesterday evening that at least two members who voted to retain Morcroft did so based on his sworn testimony that he had no private dealings with House Vanthorn prior to the exile proceedings. 'He looked us in the eye and lied,' Ashby said, her voice steady but cold. 'If this document is authentic—and every indication suggests it is—then the Chancellor committed perjury before the full Council. There is no path forward that includes him remaining in office.'

The agreement's most troubling provision appears in its third clause, which extends immunity not only to House Vanthorn's named members but to 'all agents, assignees, and financial intermediaries acting on behalf of House interests.' Legal scholars I consulted believe this language could theoretically shield anyone involved in the shell company networks currently under investigation for the Crown Reserve withdrawals and the counterfeit power cell scheme—if those networks can be traced to Vanthorn connections. I have submitted formal questions to Morcroft's office regarding whether he was aware of any such connections when he signed the agreement. As of publication, I have received no response.

High Regent Isolde Marcen has scheduled a closed-session Council meeting for tomorrow morning. My sources within the Quorum indicate she is weighing whether to invoke her emergency authority to compel Morcroft's testimony under truth-binding oath—a measure not used against a sitting Chancellor since Year 789 AP. When I reached Marcen's office for comment, her deputy secretary would say only that 'the High Regent takes all matters of Council integrity with appropriate seriousness.' Morcroft himself was seen entering the foreign affairs bureau late yesterday evening and had not emerged by midnight. His sister-in-law, Deputy Foreign Affairs Secretary Calla Stormweave, declined my request for comment through a bureau spokesperson.

The question now before Ardia is not merely whether Chancellor Morcroft lied—the document answers that question clearly enough. The question is what else that lie was designed to conceal, and how many other agreements bearing his signature remain buried in archives we have not yet searched. I intend to find out.
