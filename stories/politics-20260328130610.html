High Regent Marcen's Emergency Powers Set to Expire at Midnight With No Renewal Vote Scheduled

Three suspended Council committees could resume investigations into Crown Reserve withdrawals within hours. The Regent's office has not requested an extension.

LS
By Lyra Stormweave Senior Political Correspondent
28 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

High Regent Isolde Marcen's emergency authority—invoked four days ago to suspend three Grand Council committees investigating irregular withdrawals from the Crown Reserve—expires at the stroke of midnight tonight, and as of this evening's close of business, her office has filed no request for renewal. The silence from the Regent's chambers has left Council members, investigators, and this correspondent with the same question: did Marcen invoke emergency powers to protect the realm, or to buy time for something we have not yet uncovered?

The emergency suspension halted the work of the Fiscal Oversight Committee, the Reserve Accountability Panel, and the Special Investigation into Crown Financial Irregularities at the precise moment all three were converging on the same set of sealed documents. Those documents, according to my sources within the Council—sources I have cultivated over two decades and who have never steered me wrong—concern a series of withdrawals from the Crown Reserve totaling more than 400,000 Crowns, authorized under Chancellor Morcroft's signature but disbursed to accounts that no longer appear in Treasury records.

Councillor Maren Ashby of the Reform Coalition has already announced that her committee will convene an emergency session at dawn if the suspension lapses. 'The High Regent had four days to explain why investigating potential financial crimes against the realm constituted such a threat that she needed to shut down three separate oversight bodies,' Ashby told me outside Council chambers this evening, her voice carrying the controlled fury I have come to recognize in reformers who smell blood. 'She offered no explanation. We will not wait for one.'

The Expansionist faction, typically aligned with emergency measures in the name of stability, has been conspicuously quiet. Lord Commander Severin Drath declined my request for comment through his aide, who noted only that the Lord Commander was 'monitoring developments.' That silence speaks loudly in a Council where Drath has never hesitated to defend executive authority—unless, perhaps, that authority has been used in ways that implicate interests he would prefer remain unexamined.

I have covered Isolde Marcen since her appointment as Deputy Regent in Year 843 AP. She is not a woman who acts without calculation, nor one who surrenders authority carelessly. If she allows these powers to lapse without a fight, it is because she has concluded that fighting would cost more than losing. What she knows—what she learned in the four days those committees were silenced—may prove more significant than any document the investigators eventually recover. The clock is running. By morning, we will know whether the committees find what they were looking for, or whether someone used these four days to ensure they never do.
