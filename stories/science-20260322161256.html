Arcane Academy's Ley-Line Project Concealed Evidence of Massive Energy Drain Beneath Eastern Ardia

Leaked internal documents reveal researchers identified anomalous readings months before Greyward emergence. Director claims data was 'under review.'

TB
By Theron Blackstone Science & Arcane Research Correspondent
22 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

I have spent four months investigating irregularities in the Arcane Academy's Ley-Line Mapping Project. Last night, a source within the research division provided me with documents that transform suspicion into certainty: Academy researchers detected catastrophic energy drainage beneath eastern Ardia in late autumn of last year — three full months before the Greyward blight consumed the Miravel Woods, before the River Veyla's magical collapse, before the Whispering Stones of Keldara fell silent after four millennia.

The documents include seventeen internal memoranda from Project Director Aldric Fenworth to the Academy's Research Oversight Committee, each marked with the crimson seal indicating restricted circulation. The earliest, dated the fourteenth of Harvest Moon, describes 'unprecedented ley-current attenuation along the eastern measurement grid' and recommends immediate field investigation. The recommendation was denied. Subsequent memoranda grow increasingly urgent, with Fenworth writing on the third of Frost Fall: 'We are witnessing the magical equivalent of arterial hemorrhage. Something is drawing power from the land itself at a rate I lack theoretical framework to explain.' That memorandum was returned with a single annotation in Director of Research Castellan Vorn's handwriting: 'Revise methodology before publishing.'

When I confronted Arcanist Selene Whitewater — my former research advisor and a member of the mapping project's senior staff — she confirmed the documents' authenticity with visible reluctance. 'Aldric was right,' she told me, her hands trembling around her teacup. 'We all knew he was right. The readings were unmistakable. But Vorn wanted additional verification cycles before we made any public statements, and by the time those cycles concluded...' She gestured toward the window, toward the east, where the Greyward continues its inexorable spread. 'By then, people were already dying.'

Director Vorn declined my interview request through his secretary, but issued a written statement defending the Academy's 'rigorous commitment to scientific accuracy over premature speculation.' He characterized the suppressed memoranda as 'preliminary findings requiring substantial refinement' and noted that the Academy 'cannot be held responsible for natural phenomena we did not create.' When I asked whether the Academy's silence had cost lives — whether the highland clans might have relocated earlier, whether the Circle of Thorns might have mobilized sooner — his office did not respond.

What haunts me most is a passage from Fenworth's final suppressed memorandum, written just twelve days before the first Greyward sighting. He had mapped the drainage pattern to its apparent center: a point approximately forty leagues east of the Keldara Highlands, in a region his charts label only as 'the Thornmark Depths' — a name that appears in no Academy geography text I can locate. 'Something is there,' Fenworth wrote. 'Something is feeding.' I have submitted formal requests to the Academy's cartographic archives for any historical references to the Thornmark Depths. Those requests have been denied on grounds of 'archival preservation protocols.' I find myself wondering what else our premier institution of magical learning has decided we are not ready to know — and whether we will survive their caution long enough to find out.
