Arcane Academy's Emergency Ley-Line Sensors Detect 'Impossible' Energy Signature Beneath Praxis Itself

Reading suggests massive magical structure exists under the capital — one that predates the city's founding by centuries.

TB
By Theron Blackstone Science & Arcane Research Correspondent
23 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

The Arcane Academy's hastily deployed network of ley-line monitoring stations detected something yesterday evening that has sent the institution's theoretical spell-craft department into what one researcher described as "productive panic." At approximately the Hour of Amber Light, seventeen sensors across the greater Praxis metropolitan area simultaneously registered an energy signature emanating from roughly two thousand feet beneath the Grand Council chambers — a signature that matches no known magical phenomenon in Academy records, but which displays mathematical properties suggesting artificial construction.

"The pattern is geometric," said Arcanist Selene Whitewater, my former research advisor and one of the realm's foremost experts on ley-line dynamics, who agreed to review the preliminary data at my request. "Natural ley-line convergences follow organic curves, like river tributaries joining a stream. This signature has angles. Corners. Whatever is down there, someone built it — and based on the energy decay patterns, they built it at least eight hundred years before Praxis was founded." She paused, adjusted her spectacles in a gesture I recognized from our laboratory days, and added: "Which raises rather significant questions about why anyone would build something that elaborate in what was supposedly empty wilderness."

The detection occurred because of equipment installed in response to the Greyward Anomaly crisis. Following my reporting on the Academy's suppression of ley-line disruption data, Archmage Velindra Ashcroft authorized emergency deployment of forty-three deep-resonance sensors throughout the capital — officially to ensure Praxis faced no similar energy drain. Those sensors, it appears, found something no one was looking for. The subterranean structure appears to sit directly atop the largest natural ley-line convergence point on the continent, a location I now understand was deliberately chosen when the Grand Council established the capital in Year 0 AP. Academy historians are scrambling to determine whether the founders knew what lay beneath them.

Project Director Aldric Femworth, whose selective publication of Greyward data I exposed last month, has been notably absent from public briefings on this discovery. When I visited the Academy's Theoretical Spell-Craft wing this morning, three junior researchers independently mentioned that Femworth had requested all historical records pertaining to "pre-founding site surveys" be transferred to his private office. The Academy's archive director, who asked not to be named, confirmed this transfer occurred but declined to characterize its purpose. I have submitted a formal records request under the Press Act of Year 712.

The timing concerns me. The ley-line network across eastern Ardia is draining at triple expected rates. Crystal power cells are failing. The Breathing Ground spreads through the Thornback Mountains. And now we discover that Praxis itself sits atop an ancient magical structure of unknown purpose, built by unknown hands, drawing on the same energy network that appears to be destabilizing across the realm. I do not yet know whether these phenomena are connected. But I have learned, after twelve years at this newspaper, that coincidences of this magnitude rarely are. Gerald — my luminous moss, for those unfamiliar — has begun pulsing in a rhythm I have not observed before. He started the night the sensors detected the signature. I have no scientific explanation for this. I am including it anyway, because at this point, I believe we need all the data we can gather.
