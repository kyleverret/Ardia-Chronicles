Arcane Academy Detects Second 'Impossible' Energy Signature—This One Moving Toward Praxis

Researchers confirm the anomaly beneath Greyward has begun traveling along deep ley-lines at seventeen miles per day. Academy Director refuses comment.

TB
By Theron Blackstone Science & Arcane Research Correspondent
23 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

The energy signature that Arcane Academy sensors first detected beneath Greyward three weeks ago is no longer stationary. According to documents I obtained from sources within the Academy's Ley-Line Monitoring Division, the anomaly began moving northwest along deep ley-line channels on the evening of 21 Thawbreak—and its trajectory, if unchanged, will bring it directly beneath Praxis within eighteen days. The Academy has not issued any public statement regarding this development, and Director of Research Aldric Femworth declined my request for comment through his assistant, citing 'ongoing calibration concerns with detection equipment.'

I brought the documents to my former research advisor, Arcanist Selene Whitewater, who spent four hours reviewing the data before she would speak on the record. 'The signature is consistent with what we detected in Greyward,' she told me, her voice carefully controlled in a way I have learned to recognize after fifteen years of friendship. 'But moving signatures of this magnitude do not occur naturally. Ley-lines carry energy, yes—but energy does not travel with purpose. This is traveling with purpose.' When I asked her to clarify what she meant, she removed her spectacles and cleaned them for a very long time before answering: 'Something is using the ley-line network as a road.'

The monitoring station data reveals additional details the Academy has not disclosed. The signature's movement coincides precisely with the moment three Thornwood mother-trees uprooted themselves and began walking south—a correlation that Whitewater called 'deeply troubling but not coincidental.' Furthermore, the anomaly's rate of travel has increased by approximately three miles per day since monitoring began, suggesting either acceleration or growth. Crystal power cells within a twelve-mile radius of the signature's current position have begun draining at rates exceeding their normal output capacity, consistent with the energy drain patterns CrystalCore researchers documented in Greyward before evacuating their facility.

I visited the Academy's primary detection chamber yesterday evening, where I observed technicians working in shifts around equipment that has clearly not been powered down in days. Three separate monitoring crystals displayed the same pulsing red signature—larger than when I first reported on the Greyward anomaly, and unmistakably closer to the capital. When I asked the supervising technician whether emergency protocols had been activated, she asked me to leave and said nothing else. The Ley-Line Monitoring Division has requisitioned additional detection equipment from the Praxis municipal stores, according to procurement records filed with the Grand Council's Infrastructure Committee—equipment typically used only during major arcane emergencies.

High Regent Marcen's office acknowledged receiving a briefing from Academy officials but declined to characterize its contents. The Grand Council's Emergency Preparedness Committee has scheduled a closed session for tomorrow morning—its first such meeting since the Shadow Legion's initial incursion in Year 844 AP. Whatever is moving beneath eastern Ardia, whatever drove the mother-trees to abandon soil they had anchored for millennia, it is coming here. And the people responsible for understanding it are either unable or unwilling to tell us what it is. I will continue reporting as information becomes available. Gerald, my luminous moss, has begun pulsing in a pattern I have never observed before. I do not know what that means. I suspect I will find out soon enough.
