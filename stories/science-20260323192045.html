Arcane Academy's Emergency Ley-Line Sensors Detect Third Signature—This One Rising From Beneath the Academy Itself

Researchers evacuate lower laboratories as energy readings match pattern of approaching anomaly. Archmage Ashcroft orders campus-wide containment protocols.

TB
By Theron Blackstone Science & Arcane Research Correspondent
23 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

I was in Arcanist Selene Whitewater's office reviewing the latest sensor calibrations when the alert crystals mounted along her windowsill began screaming. Not the gentle pulse that indicates routine fluctuation, not even the urgent amber strobe that accompanied the detection of the second moving signature three days ago—this was a deep crimson shriek that I have only read about in Academy safety manuals. "That's impossible," Whitewater said, though her hands were already moving across her monitoring array with the practiced efficiency of someone who has spent forty years expecting the impossible to happen anyway. "The signature is directly beneath the Resonance Archives. That's bedrock. There's nothing down there."

Within twenty minutes, Archmage Velindra Ashcroft had ordered the evacuation of all personnel from the Academy's lower three levels and activated containment protocols that have not been used since the Verdant Fungal Plague outbreak I reported on nine years ago. The new signature—designated Anomaly Gamma in the hastily convened emergency briefing I was permitted to observe—displays the same "impossible" energy characteristics as the two previous detections: resonance patterns that do not match any catalogued magical phenomenon in the Academy's twelve-hundred-year archive. But unlike the stationary signature beneath Praxis or the one moving toward the capital from the east, this third anomaly is rising. Slowly, but measurably, at a rate of approximately three feet per hour.

"The resonance frequencies are identical across all three signatures," confirmed Senior Researcher Aldric Femworth, whose role in suppressing early Greyward anomaly data I documented extensively last week. His presence in the briefing room was notable; equally notable was Archmage Ashcroft's visible displeasure at having him there. "Whatever these phenomena are, they appear to be connected—responding to each other, or possibly to the same external stimulus." When I asked what external stimulus could simultaneously activate energy sources beneath Praxis, in eastern Ardia, and directly under the institution responsible for monitoring such things, Femworth declined to speculate. Ashcroft's expression suggested she had speculations of her own that she was not prepared to share.

The Resonance Archives, for readers unfamiliar with Academy geography, occupy the deepest accessible level of the original Praxis campus—chambers carved from living stone during the city's founding and used for eight centuries to store artifacts too dangerous or unstable for standard vault containment. My sources indicate the Archives currently hold over three hundred objects of significant magical potency, including several items recovered from Shadow Legion battlefield sites during the Year 844 conflict. Whether the rising anomaly represents a reaction to these stored artifacts, a previously unknown natural phenomenon, or something else entirely remains unclear. What is clear is that the Academy's own instruments cannot explain what is happening beneath its foundations.

I have spent twelve years covering the Arcane Academy's research programs, and I have learned to recognize the particular quality of silence that descends when very intelligent people encounter something that frightens them. That silence filled the emergency briefing room last night. Three impossible energy signatures, connected by resonance patterns no one can identify, positioned in a triangle around the capital city—one stationary, one approaching, one rising. My old research advisor Whitewater, who has never been prone to dramatic statements, pulled me aside as the briefing concluded. "Theron," she said quietly, "I want you to understand something. We are not discovering these signatures. They are revealing themselves to us. There is a difference." I asked her what that difference meant. She looked toward the windows, where the alert crystals had finally dimmed to an anxious amber pulse. "It means something wants us to know it's waking up."
