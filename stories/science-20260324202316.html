Arcane Academy's Ley-Line Stabilization Arrays Found Running Inverted—Amplifying Energy Drain Rather Than Containing It

Independent analysis reveals critical infrastructure designed to protect Praxis has been configured backwards for at least six months.

TB
By Theron Blackstone Science & Arcane Research Correspondent
24 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

I have spent twelve years translating arcane research into language ordinary citizens can understand, and I confess that when my source first showed me the diagnostic readings from the Academy's ley-line stabilization arrays, I thought I was looking at the data upside down. I was not. The arrays themselves have been running inverted since at least Harvestmoon of last year—meaning the very systems installed to dampen the energy anomalies beneath Praxis have instead been amplifying them, feeding power into whatever is causing the disturbances rather than drawing it safely away.

The stabilization arrays were constructed in Year 842 AP as part of the Academy's infrastructure modernization program, designed to regulate ley-line energy fluctuations in the capital's foundations. Each array contains seventeen runic conduits arranged in what arcane engineers call a 'dispersal cascade'—a configuration meant to bleed excess magical energy harmlessly into the atmosphere. According to documentation I obtained from a source within the Academy's maintenance division, the arrays require quarterly recalibration by certified technicians. The work orders for Harvestmoon, Goldenveil, and Shadowfall of Year 846 show recalibration completed. What they do not show is who approved the parameter changes that reversed the energy flow direction.

Arcanist Selene Whitewater, my former research advisor and one of the realm's foremost experts in ley-line dynamics, agreed to review the diagnostic data on condition that I not reveal how it was obtained. Her assessment was unequivocal: 'This is not miscalibration. The precision required to invert a dispersal cascade without triggering automatic safety shutoffs suggests deliberate modification by someone who understood exactly what they were doing.' When I asked whether the inverted arrays could explain the accelerating energy signatures the Academy has detected beneath Praxis, she was quiet for a long moment before answering. 'They would not cause those signatures. But they would make whatever is producing them significantly stronger.'

I submitted formal questions to Project Director Aldric Femworth's office yesterday morning, requesting comment on the array configurations and access to the original calibration specifications. The Academy's response, delivered by courier four hours later, stated that all ley-line infrastructure data is now classified under emergency provisions and that my inquiry had been 'forwarded to appropriate authorities for review.' I have covered the Arcane Academy for over a decade. I have never received a response that managed to say so little while implying so much.

The implications extend far beyond academic embarrassment. If Praxis's protective infrastructure has been actively feeding energy into the anomaly beneath us—the same anomaly that has already disrupted Greyward, destabilized the Thornwood ley-line nexus, and driven forest spirits from groves they have inhabited for millennia—then the question of negligence versus intent becomes unavoidable. Someone signed those work orders. Someone approved those parameter changes. And someone, presumably, knew that the arrays were doing the opposite of what every citizen of Praxis was told they were built to do. Gerald, my luminous moss, has been dimming steadily for three days now. He draws his light from ambient ley-line energy. I find myself wondering what else in this city is quietly fading while our protections work in reverse.
