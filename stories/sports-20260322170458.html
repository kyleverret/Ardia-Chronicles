Ashfield Cup Jousting Champion Withdraws Hours Before Title Defense, Cites 'Threats Against Family'

Sir Aldric Stonebridge becomes first defending champion to forfeit in tournament's 200-year history. Organizing committee launches immediate investigation.

GI
By Gareth Ironwood Sports & Tournaments Correspondent
22 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

I have covered the Ashfield Cup for eleven years, watched champions rise and legends fall, and never have I seen anything like what happened this morning. Sir Aldric Stonebridge — the finest lance in Ardia, the man who unseated three former champions in last year's tournament without taking a single point against him — walked into the organizing committee's pavilion at dawn, placed his silver champion's medallion on the table, and withdrew from competition without explanation beyond five words: "They will hurt my daughters."

The committee, whose members I have known since my boyhood watching tournaments from my mother's inn window, immediately sealed the pavilion and summoned the City Watch. I was able to speak with Stonebridge for perhaps two minutes before his handlers escorted him away. His hands were steady — this is a man who has charged armored opponents at full gallop without flinching — but his voice was not. "Someone delivered a message to my home in Praxis last night," he told me. "They knew which rooms my children sleep in. They knew their names. They told me exactly what would happen if I competed today." He would say nothing more about the message's contents or its source.

Tournament Director Mira Ashford — no relation to the Ashfield Plains' founding family despite the name — convened an emergency session within the hour. The Cup has never been forfeited by a defending champion in its two-hundred-year history. Protocol simply does not exist for this situation. After heated deliberation, the committee ruled that runner-up Sir Bram Castellan of Valdris will face the qualifying winner in the championship bracket, effectively erasing Stonebridge's title defense from the record. Castellan, to his credit, refused to celebrate. "This isn't how any of us want to win," he told me. "Something is very wrong here."

I spent the afternoon digging through my contacts on the organizing committee, including sources who have provided me with pre-competition injury assessments for years. What I learned troubles me deeply. Three other competitors — all seeded in the top eight — received similar visits last night. Two withdrew this morning citing sudden illness; the third, Dame Sera Thornwood of Ironkeep, competed anyway and was unhorsed in the first pass by a rider she had defeated four times previously. When I found her behind the medical pavilion afterward, she was shaking. "I couldn't focus," she admitted. "Every time I lowered my lance, I saw my brother's face." Her brother serves with the Grand Army at the Shadow Frontier.

The pattern here is unmistakable, and it echoes something I have been tracking for months in a different arena entirely. The Grand Mage Duels — where Chief Adjudicator Brennan resigned just days ago amid evidence of systematic match manipulation — showed the same statistical fingerprints before the scandal broke: unexpected withdrawals, improbable upsets, champions performing far below their capabilities. Someone is corrupting Ardia's competitive tournaments, and they have now moved from magical dueling to the realm's most prestigious athletic event. My sources within the Ashfield Cup's financial office confirm that betting volume on this tournament exceeded last year's by over sixty percent, with unusually large wagers placed on specific upset outcomes in the forty-eight hours before competition began.

I knew Aldric Stonebridge before he was a champion. I watched him train as a young knight, saw the dedication that carried him from a minor house to the silver medallion. He is not a coward. Whatever threat compelled him to walk away from glory was real enough to break a man who has never broken before. The Ashfield Cup organizing committee has requested assistance from Magistrate Thornwood's office — the same court currently sheltering witnesses in the Vanthorn smuggling case under temple sanctuary. Someone powerful enough to threaten champions' families, coordinate attacks across multiple competitors, and move massive sums through the betting markets is poisoning Ardian sport. I intend to find out who.
