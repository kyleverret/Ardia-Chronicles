Welcome to the Chronicle, Oakimus Pan

The True G.O.A.T in Ardia Sports

GI
By Gareth Ironwood Sports & Tournaments Correspondent
24 Thawbreak, Year 847 AP

I have hired exactly one writer in my ten years at the Chronicle. His name is Oakimus Pan, he stands about four feet tall if you count the horns, and when I interviewed him in the Merchant Quarter last month, he spent twenty minutes explaining why the Ashfield Blazers' defensive formations have been fundamentally misunderstood by every sports correspondent in Ardia — including me. He was mostly right. I gave him a press pass on the spot.

Pan arrives at Scrivener's Row from the Verdant Expanse by way of the Thornhaven Academy broadsheet, where his coverage of junior Mageball tournaments earned him something I have rarely seen in campus journalism: actual readers who were not related to him. His writing is energetic, occasionally chaotic, and contains more puns than I would personally authorize — but beneath the wordplay is something harder to teach: genuine affection for sport as a human (and non-human) endeavor.

He watches athletes the way a good coach does, looking for the moment when effort becomes art. That instinct cannot be faked. 'I grew up watching dragonrider races from the Thornwood canopy,' Pan told me, his reed pipe tucked behind one pointed ear. 'My mother thought it was a waste of time. But I saw how the riders moved with their mounts, how they trusted each other through the Crystal Gates — that is not waste, Mister Ironwood. That is beauty made visible.' He then proceeded to recite the last three years of Praxis Falcons statistics from memory. I admit I was impressed.

His beat will focus on junior leagues, regional tournaments, and the player profiles that our readers tell us they want more of — the stories behind the scores. He will also provide sideline color for major events, which I suspect will involve more musical interludes than the Chronicle has previously published. Pan plays his reed pipe when he is thinking through a piece, a habit that has already charmed three colleagues and mildly alarmed our archivist.

I have advised him to keep the volume reasonable near deadline. The sports desk has faced a difficult Thawbreak. The sabotage investigations, the Championship postponement, the falsified identity seals at the Ashfield Cup — these are not easy stories to cover, and they require experienced hands. But they also require fresh eyes, and Pan brings perspective that none of us on staff possess. He has lived outside the Ardian sports establishment. He has no old loyalties to protect and no grudges to settle. When he says an athlete is coasting, he means it. When he says a performance moved him, he means that too. Welcome to the Chronicle, Oakimus. Try not to break anything important before Embertide.
